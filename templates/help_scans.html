Quick Start

In 3 simple steps:

  1. Enter the website URL you want to scan
  2. Click "Start Accessibility Scan"
  3. Wait for the scan to complete and view results
Tip: Most websites can be scanned with the default settings. Advanced options are available if you need more control.

Running Your First Scan

Step 1: Enter Website URL

On the home page, enter the full URL of the website you want to scan in the "Website URL" field.

Example: https://example.com
Important: Include the protocol (http:// or https://) in the URL.

Step 2: Choose Scan Settings (Optional)

You can customize these settings or use the defaults:

  • Max Pages to Scan: How many pages to scan (default: 50, max: 10,000)
  • Max Depth: How deep to crawl from the starting URL (default: 3)
  • Restrict to Path: Only scan pages within the starting URL path (enabled by default)
  • Capture Screenshots: Optional - saves screenshots but significantly increases scan time
Note: The accessibility checks performed are based on your personalized "My Checks" configuration. Customize which checks are enabled from the My Checks page.

Step 3: Start the Scan

Click the "Start Accessibility Scan" button. The scan will begin immediately.

Step 4: Monitor Progress

You'll see a real-time progress indicator showing:

  • Number of pages scanned
  • Violations found
  • Estimated time remaining
  • Progress percentage

Step 5: View Results

When the scan completes, you'll be automatically redirected to the results page where you can:

  • View all violations found
  • See violations organized by page
  • Export results as Word or Excel
  • Print the report

Scan Configuration Options

Max Pages to Scan

What it does: Limits the number of pages scanned

Default: 50 pages

Range: 1 to 10,000 pages

Use case: Increase for larger sites, decrease for quick tests

Max Depth

What it does: Controls how deep the crawler goes from the starting URL

Default: 3 levels deep

Range: 1 to 10 levels

Example: Depth 1 only scans pages linked directly from the homepage

Restrict to Path

What it does: Limits scanning to pages within the starting URL's path

Default: Enabled (checked)

Example: Starting at https://example.com/blog will only scan /blog pages

Use case: Focus scans on specific website sections

Capture Screenshots

What it does: Saves screenshots of each scanned page

Default: Disabled (unchecked)

Warning: Significantly increases scan time

Use case: Visual documentation or detailed reports

Pro Tip: For comprehensive audits, use Max Pages: 500+ and Max Depth: 5+. For quick spot checks, use Max Pages: 10-25 and Max Depth: 2-3.

Customizing Accessibility Checks

The specific accessibility checks performed during a scan are controlled by your personalized check configuration:

  • Go to "My Checks" in the navigation menu
  • Enable or disable individual accessibility checks
  • Set severity levels (Error, Warning, Alert) for each check
  • Your settings apply to all your scans automatically
Personalized Scanning: Each user can customize which accessibility checks to run, allowing you to focus on what matters most for your website.

Monitoring Scan Progress

Real-Time Updates

While a scan is running, you'll see:

  • Pages Scanned: Number of pages analyzed so far
  • Issues Found: Total violations detected
  • Progress Bar: Visual indication of completion percentage
  • Est. Time Remaining: Approximate time until completion

Scan Checkpointing

Scans automatically save progress every 50 pages. If a scan is interrupted:

  • Progress is saved to the database
  • You can resume from the last checkpoint
  • Go to History page and click "Resume" on the interrupted scan
Protection: Browser refresh or network issues won't lose your scan progress.

Best Practices & Tips

For your first scan, use a small page limit (10-25) to get familiar with the results. Once comfortable, increase to scan the entire site.

For large scans (500+ pages), run them during off-peak hours to minimize impact on your web server and get faster results.

Scan your website regularly (weekly or monthly) to catch accessibility issues early. Use the History page to track improvements over time.

Use the "My Checks" feature to enable/disable specific accessibility checks based on your needs. Focus on what matters most for your site.

After each scan, export results to Word or Excel format. Share with your development team for remediation tracking.

Common Issues

Q: The scan is taking a long time

A: Large websites naturally take longer. Check the estimated time remaining. You can also reduce Max Pages or Max Depth for faster scans.

Q: Some pages aren't being scanned

A: Pages might be behind authentication, use JavaScript-heavy navigation, or be outside the Max Depth. Try increasing Max Depth or manually scanning specific sections.

Q: Can I scan password-protected sites?

A: Currently, the scanner cannot access password-protected pages. Consider scanning public pages or use URL Discovery to map out accessible sections first.
